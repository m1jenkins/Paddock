Top 5 Driving Roads in the Texas Hill Country

By Provenance Auto Advisory · March 10, 2026 · 7 min read

Texas Has World-Class Driving Roads

Texas may be known for its vast, flat expanses, but the Hill Country west and northwest of Austin contains some of the finest driving roads in the American South. Winding through limestone hills, past crystal-clear rivers, and through charming small towns, these roads are tailor-made for exotic sports cars, grand tourers, and classic convertibles. If you own a special car in Texas — or are thinking about buying one — these five routes should be at the top of your list.

1. The Twisted Sisters (Ranch Roads 335, 336, 337)

The legendary Twisted Sisters — also known as the Three Sisters — form a roughly 100-mile loop through some of the most dramatic terrain in the Hill Country, starting and ending near Leakey, Texas. Ranch Road 337, the crown jewel, features tight switchbacks, elevation changes, and sweeping vistas that rival mountain passes found anywhere in the world. Best experienced in a Porsche 911 GT3, Ferrari Roma, or any car that rewards precise inputs. The route is approximately 2-3 hours round trip and can be combined with lunch in Leakey or Vanderpool.

2. Lime Creek Road (FM 1431)

Lime Creek Road is Austin's home track — a 6-mile stretch of tight, technical curves that winds through the hills above Lake Travis. Just 30 minutes from downtown Austin, it's the go-to road for local car enthusiasts and the centerpiece of many weekend group drives. The road is best experienced early in the morning when traffic is light. Perfect for: Porsche Cayman GT4, BMW M2, McLaren Artura, or any nimble sports car that rewards quick reflexes.

3. Highway 16 (Kerrville to Bandera)

Highway 16 between Kerrville and Bandera is a 25-mile stretch of sweeping, high-speed curves through classic Hill Country ranch land. The road is wider and faster than the Twisted Sisters, making it ideal for grand tourers and high-performance luxury cars. Think Bentley Continental GT, Mercedes-AMG GT, or Ferrari Portofino with the top down. The route passes through Medina, the 'Apple Capital of Texas,' where you can stop for fresh cider.

4. Devil's Backbone (FM 32)

Devil's Backbone runs along a dramatic ridge between Wimberley and Canyon Lake, offering panoramic views of the Hill Country that stretch for miles. The road features a mix of tight technical sections and flowing curves, with some of the best scenery in Central Texas. Located between Austin and San Antonio, it's easily accessible from both cities. Best enjoyed in a convertible or targa — a Porsche 911 Targa, Ferrari 296 GTS, or classic Mercedes-Benz SL.

5. Willow City Loop

The Willow City Loop, northeast of Fredericksburg, is famous for its wildflower displays in spring, but it's a fantastic driving road year-round. The 13-mile loop winds through narrow valleys, past granite outcroppings, and along scenic ranch land. The road is relatively gentle — perfect for a relaxed drive in a luxury grand tourer or classic car. Just remember: the loop is one-lane in places, so take it easy and enjoy the scenery. After the drive, downtown Fredericksburg offers excellent German restaurants, wine tasting, and shopping.
