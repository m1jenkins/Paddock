Ferrari Allocation Guide: What Texas Buyers Need to Know

By Provenance Auto Advisory · March 18, 2026 · 10 min read

The Ferrari Allocation System Explained

Ferrari's allocation system is perhaps the most exclusive — and most misunderstood — in the automotive world. Unlike most manufacturers where you can simply order a car, Ferrari controls exactly who gets to buy their vehicles through a careful allocation process managed by Ferrari North America and individual authorized dealers. Understanding this system is essential for any Texas buyer hoping to own a new Prancing Horse.

How Allocations Work in Texas

Texas has several authorized Ferrari dealers, including Ferrari of Houston, Ferrari of Dallas, Ferrari of Austin, and Ferrari of San Antonio. Each dealer receives a limited number of allocations for new models based on their sales performance and relationship with Ferrari North America. These allocations are then distributed to customers based on several factors: purchase history with the dealer, loyalty to the brand, the specific model requested, and the dealer's discretion. For highly limited models like the SF90 XX Stradale or the Daytona SP3, only the most loyal customers with extensive purchase histories will be considered.

Getting Your First Ferrari in Texas

If you've never purchased a Ferrari before, getting your first one requires strategy. The most accessible entry point is typically a Ferrari Roma, Portofino M, or 296 GTB — models with higher production numbers. Approach a Texas Ferrari dealer, express genuine interest in the brand, and be prepared to wait. Some dealers require a deposit to be placed on a waitlist. Working with a consultant like Provenance can significantly accelerate this process, as we have established relationships with Ferrari dealers and understand which paths are most likely to succeed for first-time buyers.

The Purchase History Ladder

Ferrari rewards brand loyalty with access to increasingly exclusive models. A typical progression might look like: Roma or Portofino (entry) → 296 GTB → F8 Tributo → 812 Competizione → SF90 Stradale → limited series models. Each purchase builds your history and positions you for the next allocation. This is a long-term relationship, not a one-time transaction. Provenance helps Texas clients develop a strategic approach to building their Ferrari purchase history efficiently.

Pre-Owned Ferraris: An Alternative Path

For buyers who don't want to navigate the allocation process, the certified pre-owned Ferrari market offers an excellent alternative. Recent models like the F8 Tributo, SF90, and 296 GTB are available in the pre-owned market, often at or near their original MSRP. Pre-owned also gives you access to beloved older models — the 458 Italia, 488 GTB, California T, and the iconic F430.
